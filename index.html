Max Lyapunov λ — measures exponential divergence of nearby trajectories. A positive value means tiny differences in initial conditions grow exponentially over time, the hallmark of chaos. Values near zero or negative indicate regular (non-chaotic) motion. Estimated by rerunning the simulation with a 10⁻⁷ perturbation and fitting the log-separation slope.
Correlation dimension — estimates the fractal dimension of the attractor. Integer values (1 = limit cycle, 2 = torus) indicate regular motion. Non-integer values (e.g. 1.3 or 2.06) indicate fractal, self-similar structure — a strange attractor. Computed via the Grassberger-Procaccia algorithm on the (θ, P, L) trajectory.
Energy drift — the Hamiltonian (T + V) should be conserved. Large drift indicates numerical error from the integrator. Reduce dt or simulation time if drift is large. Values below 10⁻² are generally acceptable.
Note on projections — this plots a 3D slice of a 2n-dimensional phase space. Use the axis selectors to choose which variables to plot. Try combinations like (θᵢ, Pᵢ, θⱼ) or (θᵢ, Pᵢ, Pⱼ) for different pendulums to reveal attractor structure that the Lagrangian view might hide.
